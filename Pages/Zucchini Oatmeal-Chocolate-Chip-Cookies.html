Zucchini Oatmeal Chocolate Chip Cookies
  • Level: Easy
  • Total: 40 min
  • Active: 40 min
  • Yield: 8 to 10 servings

    • Active: 40 min

The cosmopolitan, a 90s favorite, meets the 50s classic ginger ale punch in this tasty mash-up. A gorgeous frozen fruit ring keeps the punch cold long into the night. If you can't find fresh cranberries, frozen will work as well.





Watch Tutorial


Cajun Butter:

  1. 2 sticks (1 cup) unsalted butter, at room temperature
  2. 2 teaspoons paprika
  3. 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  4. 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  5. 1 teaspoon onion powder
  6. 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  7. 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  8. Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper Miso Butter:
  9. 2 sticks (1 cup) unsalted butter, at room temperature
  10. 3 tablespoons white miso
  11. 1 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes
  12. 1 teaspoon rice wine vinegar
  13. Finely grated zest and juice of 1 lime
  14. Kosher salt
  15. Toasted sesame seeds, for serving Green Goddess Butter:
  16. 2 sticks (1 cup) unsalted butter, at room temperature
  17. 1/2 cup lightly packed fresh cilantro leaves
  18. 1/2 cup lightly packed fresh parsley leaves
  19. 2 scallions, roughly chopped
  20. 1 clove garlic, minced
  21. Finely grated zest and juice of 1 lemon
  22. Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper Grilled Corn:
  23. Vegetable or canola oil, for the grill grates
  24. 8 medium ears corn, shucked and cut in half crosswise

Directions

  1. For the Cajun butter: Mix the butter, paprika, cayenne, garlic powder, onion powder, oregano, thyme, 2 teaspoons salt and 1/2 teaspoon black pepper in a medium bowl until well combined. Transfer to a shallow serving dish or ramekin. Cover and refrigerate until ready to serve.



  2. For the miso butter: Mix the butter, miso, crushed red pepper flakes, rice wine vinegar, lime zest and juice and 1 teaspoon salt in a medium bowl until well combined. Transfer to a shallow serving dish or ramekin; top with a sprinkle of toasted sesame seeds. Cover and refrigerate until ready to serve.



  3. For the green goddess butter: Add the butter, cilantro, parsley, scallions, garlic, lemon zest and juice, 2 teaspoons salt and 1/2 teaspoon black pepper to a food processor. Puree, stopping and scraping down the sides as needed, until very smooth and combined. Transfer to a shallow serving dish or ramekin. Cover and refrigerate until ready to serve.



  4. For the grilled corn: Prepare a grill for medium-high heat. Brush the grates with a little oil.



  5. Grill the corn, turning occasionally, until lightly charred and tender, 8 to 10 minutes. Serve hot on a large wooden board or platter with the Cajun butter, miso butter and green goddess butter.

Cook’s Note

all 3 butters, covered, in the refrigerator for up to 4 days. Alternatively, freeze for up to 1 month.
